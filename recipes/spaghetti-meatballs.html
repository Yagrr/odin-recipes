Spaghetti and meatballs

Description

Italian-American culture summed up as a dish, consisting of spaghetti, tomato sauce and meatballs. Grated cheese topping optional but highly recommended.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (453 g) ground beef
  • 1/3 cup (20 g) breadcrumbs
  • 1/3 cup (20 g) grated pecorino or parmesan cheese, leave some for garnish
  • Milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt (or more depending on preference)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 28 oz (800 g) can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 lb (225 g) dried spaghetti
  • Parsley or basil

Steps

  1. Add ground beef, grated cheese, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper and a generous amount of black pepper into a bowl
  2. In a separate receptacle, soak breadcrumbs into milk and soak for a minute
  3. Lift breadcrumbs out of milk (squeeze milk out of crumbs for firmer meatballs) and add to meat mixture
  4. Gently mix meat mixture together with fingertips until somewhat homogenous
  5. Roll meat into balls (approx. 2 cm wide) then let it sit covered and refrigerated for a few hours (preferably overnight)
  6. Heat olive oil in a wide pan on moderate heat. Add meatballs and cook until one side is browned
  7. Carefully push meatballs off to the side to brown as much of the other side as possible, be careful not to let the meat burn
  8. Put the tomato paste in to fry for a bit, then add crushed tomatoes to deglaze the pan
  9. Stir frequently for 10-20 minutes until the sauce has been reduced according to preference
  10. Before eating, add grated cheese and freshly chopped herb into sauce then taste for salt
  11. Boil spaghetti in water until almost done (do not overcook as the spagheti continues to cook due to residual heat)
  12. Drain spaghetti and mix it in sauce and meatballs, adding some pasta water if the sauce is not liquid enough
  13. Serve on plate topped with grated cheese and fresh herb
